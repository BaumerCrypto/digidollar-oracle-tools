Cross-VLAN Firewall Rules
❌ DENYVLAN 20 (Household) → VLAN 10 (Oracle) — personal devices cannot reach oracle
❌ DENYVLAN 30 (Mining) → VLAN 10 (Oracle) — miners cannot reach oracle
❌ DENYVLAN 40 (IoT) → ANY other VLAN — IoT is completely isolated
✅ ALLOWVLAN 10 (Oracle) → Internet — P2P connections + price feeds
✅ ALLOWVLAN 30 (Mining) → Internet — stratum connections + pool access
✅ ALLOWWireGuard VPN (10.5.5.0/24) → ALL VLANs — authenticated remote management
🔷 Why This Matters
If a smart camera gets hacked, it can't reach your oracle — it's on VLAN 40, completely isolated. If your kid downloads malware on their phone, it's on VLAN 20 — no path to VLAN 10. Each VLAN is its own network segment with firewall rules enforced at the router/switch level.

WireGuard VPN is the only way to cross VLAN boundaries remotely. One UDP port (51820), invisible to scanners without the key. Connect from your phone or laptop anywhere in the world and manage your entire infrastructure.

This is based on a real production setup — battle-tested for years across mining and node infrastructure. Requires a managed switch (TP-Link, Ubiquiti, Mikrotik) and a router with VLAN + WireGuard support.
